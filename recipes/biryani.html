Biryani

Nothing's like a Biryani made the right way - The Mughlai Way.

Biryani is a flavorful mixed rice dish made with aromatic spices, basmati rice, and meat. It’s known for its rich layers of taste and fragrance, often served with raita or salan on the side.

Ingredients

  • Chicken, beef, or lamb
  • Basmati rice
  • Yogurt
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves)
  • Salt
  • Ginger Garlic paste
  • Ground spices (turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder)
  • saffron(for aroma and color)
  • Boondi
  • Onions
  • Green Chillies
  • Tomatoes

Steps

  1. Washa and soak rice: Rinse basmati rice, soak for 20–30 minutes, then parboil until about 70% cooked..
  2. Cook the meat: In a pot, heat ghee or oil, sauté onions until golden, add ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, yogurt, and spices. Cook until the meat/vegetables are tender and well-coated..
  3. Layering: In a heavy-bottom pot, spread a layer of rice, then the cooked meat/veg mixture, then more rice. Sprinkle fresh mint, coriander, fried onions, and saffron milk (if using).
  4. Dum cooking (steam): Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough around the rim) and cook on low heat for 20–30 minutes so the flavors infuse.
  5. Preparing the raita: Soak the boondi in warm water briefly, squeeze out the excess, then add it to smooth whisked yogurt seasoned with salt, roasted cumin powder, and optionally chili powder or chopped coriander, before serving chilled.
  6. Serve: Fluff gently and serve with raita & salad.
